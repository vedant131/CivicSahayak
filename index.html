How to navigate India's legal system and access free legal aid — step by step.
📋 How to File an FIR — Step by Step
1
Police Station
Go to station in whose jurisdiction the incident occurred
2
Ask SHO to register FIR — FREE, your legal right under Sec. 154 CrPC
3
If refused, approach SP/DCP or file with Magistrate under Sec. 156(3) CrPC
4
Always collect FREE copy. Note FIR number. Zero-FIR if crime outside jurisdiction.
🏛️ NALSA — Free Legal Aid
National Legal Services Authority provides free lawyers for SC/ST, women, PwD, income < ₹1L/year. Call 15100 or visit nalsa.gov.in
📱 Apply Online
Apply at nalsa.gov.in for free legal services, Lok Adalat hearings, and pre-litigation mediation. No lawyer fees.
✅ Who's Eligible?
SC/ST, women, children, PwD, trafficking victims, persons in custody, income < ₹1 lakh/year — all eligible for free aid.
⚖️ Lok Adalat
Disputes settled free of court fees. Final and binding award. Best for motor accidents, family disputes, labour cases.
📋 Writ Petition
Go directly to High Court (Art. 226) or Supreme Court (Art. 32) for fundamental rights violations. Urgent matters get speedy hearing.
🏪 Paralegal Volunteers
NALSA-trained volunteers in every district provide door-step legal guidance. Contact your District Legal Services Authority (DLSA).

NSP Scholarship Status Guide
Understanding where your application is stuck — and what to do at each stage.
📊 Application Journey — 5 Stages
1
Student Submission
You fill and submit on scholarships.gov.in. Common issues: wrong marks (enter %, not CGPA), Aadhaar name mismatch, file size >200KB.
2
Institute Nodal Officer (INO) Verification
Your college/school verifies your enrollment and documents. If stuck: Visit your accounts/scholarship cell — they may not be aware of your application.
3
District / State Nodal Officer (DNO/SNO) Verification
Government officer verifies caste/income certificates. If stuck: Visit the District Social Welfare Office with originals. Application may be "Defective" — check your dashboard.
4
Ministry Approval
Ministry of Social Justice / Minority Affairs approves. Delays common during Oct–Jan. Check NSP dashboard for "Approved" status.
5
DBT Transfer via PFMS
Money sent to your Aadhaar-linked bank account. If not received: Check Aadhaar seeding at your bank, verify IFSC/account number entered is correct, use pfms.nic.in to track.
⚠️ Top Rejection Reasons & Fixes
❌
Marks entered as CGPA instead of %
Fix: Convert using your institute's conversion formula (e.g., CGPA × 9.5 for CBSE). Enter percentage, not CGPA.
❌
Aadhaar not seeded to bank account
Fix: Go to your bank branch or use net banking → Service Requests → Link Aadhaar. Or use NPCI BASE platform (npci.org.in/what-we-do/aadhaar-seeding).
❌
Income certificate signed by wrong authority
Fix: Must be signed by SDM/Tehsildar/Circle Officer. Notary signatures are NOT accepted. Get a fresh certificate from your local SDM office.
❌
Document file size exceeds 200KB
Fix: Use ilovepdf.com or compress PDF/JPEG online. NSP only accepts PDF/JPEG under 200KB per file.
❌
Applied under wrong home state
Fix: You must apply under your HOME state (where you're domiciled), NOT the state where you study. The Application ID is tied to your home state and cannot be changed.
🆘 If Scholarship is Delayed or Rejected
1. Check status at scholarships.gov.in → your dashboard → "Application Status"
2. If "Defective" — fix the flagged issue and resubmit within the defective verification window
3. File NSP Grievance: Login → Services → Grievance Registration
4. Call NSP Helpdesk: 0120-6619540 (Mon–Fri, 10AM–6PM)
5. Email: [email protected] with your Application ID
